Module 8: Taxes and Tax Planning

Understanding Taxes

Taxes are compulsory contributions levied by governments on income, property, sales, and other transactions. Understanding the different types of taxes is essential for effective financial planning.

  • Income Tax: A tax on the earnings of individuals and businesses. It's usually progressive, meaning the rate increases as income increases.
  • Property Tax: Tax based on the value of owned property, often used to fund local services like schools and infrastructure.
  • Sales Tax: A consumption tax imposed on the sale of goods and services, typically collected at the point of sale.

For more information on understanding taxes, read this IRS guide.

Tax Deductions and Credits

Tax deductions and credits can significantly reduce your tax burden. Knowing how to leverage them is key to effective tax planning.

  • Tax Deductions: These reduce your taxable income, leading to a lower overall tax bill. Common deductions include mortgage interest, state taxes, and charitable contributions.
  • Tax Credits: These directly reduce the amount of tax owed, making them more valuable than deductions. Examples include the Earned Income Tax Credit and Child Tax Credit.
